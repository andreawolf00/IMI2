Integration of H&E and immunohistochemistry images to improve AI-based recurrence prediction in negative sentinel lymph-node melanoma patients

IHC CO-REGISTRATION

Digitalized H&E-stained whole-slide images (WSIs) were acquired at 40Ã— magnification and stored as gigapixel, multi-resolution pyramidal images to allow efficient navigation and analysis. For each WSI, two expert pathologists manually annotated two distinct Regions of Interest (ROIs) directly on the H&E image:

(i) a TUMOR ROI, containing only tumor cells, and (ii) a TUMOR + INF ROI, encompassing tumor areas with adjacent or infiltrating immune cells. To enable multi-staining analysis, all corresponding immunohistochemical (IHC) slides were spatially aligned to the H&E reference image using rigid and non-rigid image registration techniques, ensuring accurate co-localization of tissue structures across stains.
